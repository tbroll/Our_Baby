Software Testing and Development Lab

About the Lab

WSU Computer Science students staff the Software Testing and Development Lab and work to provide services to local and regional businesses and industries. These services include testing, development, along with other computer and networking related technical services. WSU Computer Science students provide these services under the direction and supervision of Computer Science faculty and staff.

Although any WSU student can work at the Software Testing Lab, Computer Science students are typically given preference. Student work hours vary during the school year and during breaks. During academic terms students may work 10 to 15 hours per week. During breaks or the Summer they work 20 to 40 hours per week.

How to Apply

Appointment Process:

Students may apply for Testing Lab positions at anytime, however students are appointed to the lab typically between the beginning of a term and midterm. Applications are kept for the next appointment cycle, but students should reapply each term if they are not immediately selected. Appointment application forms are available in the CS department office. Applications should be submitted to Dr. Cichanowski or Sue McDonnell.

Appointment Criteria

  1. Preference given to CS students
  2. Academic performance
  3. Work ethic
  4. Time to graduation
  5. Availability during summer and breaks
  6. Team working skills
  7. Communication skills
